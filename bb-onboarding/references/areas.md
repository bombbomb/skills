# BB Onboarding — Learning Areas

These are the 10 core AI learning areas used in the BombBomb AI onboarding skill. Each area has a title, tagline, and description used to brief research agents.

---

## Area 1: Prompting Like a Pro
**Tagline**: Techniques That Change Everything

**Description**: The art and science of communicating with AI to get consistently excellent results. Covers prompt structure, context setting, role prompting, zero-shot vs. few-shot techniques, chain-of-thought reasoning, iterative refinement, and how to avoid common pitfalls like vague instructions or over-constraining the output. This is the foundational skill that makes every other AI capability more powerful.

---

## Area 2: Build Your AI Workflow
**Tagline**: Chaining Tools Together for Multiplied Output

**Description**: Moving beyond single AI conversations to build repeatable, automated workflows. Covers tool chaining (using outputs of one AI tool as inputs to another), trigger-based automation with platforms like Zapier, Make, and n8n, template libraries, and how to systematize repeatable tasks so AI does the heavy lifting consistently. The difference between using AI occasionally and using it as an always-on productivity layer.

---

## Area 3: AI Agents
**Tagline**: What They Are and When to Trust Them

**Description**: Understanding autonomous AI agents — systems that can take multi-step actions, browse the web, run code, manage files, and complete complex tasks with minimal human oversight. Covers the difference between chat AI and agentic AI, how to evaluate when to trust vs. verify agent outputs, the current capabilities and limitations of agents, and how agentic systems are reshaping knowledge work. Includes tools like Claude Cowork, ChatGPT Agents, and specialized vertical agents.

---

## Area 4: Privacy, Data, and AI at Work
**Tagline**: What Everyone Needs to Know

**Description**: The critical but often overlooked dimension of AI adoption. Covers what data is safe (and not safe) to share with AI tools, enterprise AI policies, regulatory considerations (GDPR, CCPA), prompt injection risks, how AI providers use your inputs, and how to use AI confidently without creating compliance, legal, or reputational risk. Also covers data minimization principles and how to get great results without sharing sensitive information.

---

## Area 5: AI as a Creative Partner
**Tagline**: Brainstorming, Ideation, and Beating Blocks

**Description**: Using AI as a collaborative creative force rather than just a text generator. Covers brainstorming and idea expansion techniques, creative constraints as prompting tools, using AI for drafts that you then humanize, generating multiple options to spark your own thinking, overcoming creative blocks, and the human-AI creative loop (AI generates → human edits → AI refines). Includes writing, campaign ideation, problem-solving frameworks, and presentation development.

---

## Area 6: AI for Video and Communication
**Tagline**: Making Every Message Land

**Description**: AI applied specifically to the communication challenges at the heart of BombBomb's business. Covers AI-assisted video script writing, generating personalized outreach at scale (while keeping it human), follow-up sequence automation, tone and clarity editing, transcription and summarization of video content, and how to use AI to make video messaging faster without making it feel templated. This area is uniquely relevant to BombBomb's mission.

---

## Area 7: AI for Sales and Revenue
**Tagline**: Closing More with Less Effort

**Description**: AI applied to the full sales cycle. Covers lead research automation, personalized outreach generation, pipeline intelligence and forecasting, objection handling assistance, competitive research, meeting prep and follow-up, and using AI to identify signals that a prospect is ready to buy. Includes specific tools for sales teams and how to integrate AI into CRM workflows without disrupting existing processes.

---

## Area 8: AI for Data and Insights
**Tagline**: From Raw Numbers to Real Answers

**Description**: Using AI to make sense of data without needing to be a data scientist. Covers natural language data analysis (asking questions of your data in plain English), automated report generation, pattern recognition in large datasets, AI-powered dashboards, and using AI to identify trends and anomalies in business data. Includes tools like ChatGPT's data analysis mode, Claude's document analysis, and specialized BI tools with AI features.

---

## Area 9: AI Tools Landscape
**Tagline**: What's Out There and What Actually Works

**Description**: A practical survey of the AI tools ecosystem in 2025. Covers the major LLM platforms (Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity), image generation tools, voice AI, coding assistants, AI-enhanced productivity apps, and specialized vertical AI tools. Includes a framework for evaluating new AI tools, understanding model differences and when they matter, and how to avoid "tool sprawl" — using many AI tools inefficiently instead of mastering a few.

---

## Area 10: Staying Ahead
**Tagline**: The Future of AI and Your Career

**Description**: Building the habits and mindset to stay current as AI evolves rapidly. Covers how to follow AI developments without drowning in hype, how roles and skill requirements are changing, building a personal learning system for AI, when to be an early adopter vs. wait-and-see, and how to position yourself as an AI-fluent professional. Also covers the broader landscape: what AI can and can't do, where it's headed, and how to think about your career trajectory in an AI-accelerated world.
