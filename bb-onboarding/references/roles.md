# BB Onboarding — Role Priority Mapping

## Priority Order by Role

Each role has 6 priority areas (ordered 1=highest priority) and 4 bonus areas.

### Sales
Priority: 7, 1, 6, 5, 9, 3
Bonus: 2, 4, 8, 10

**Role context**: Sales reps at BombBomb are relationship builders. They live in their CRM, send lots of video messages, and are measured on pipeline and closed revenue. AI should help them research prospects faster, personalize outreach at scale, and spend more time on high-value conversations.

---

### Marketing
Priority: 5, 1, 6, 9, 2, 10
Bonus: 3, 4, 7, 8

**Role context**: Marketing owns brand, content, campaigns, and demand gen. They're often the first team to adopt AI broadly. AI should help them generate more content faster, run better campaigns, and measure what's working — while keeping BombBomb's human-first voice intact.

---

### Customer Success
Priority: 6, 1, 7, 3, 9, 4
Bonus: 2, 5, 8, 10

**Role context**: CS teams are responsible for customer retention, expansion, and health. They use video heavily for onboarding, check-ins, and escalations. AI should help them identify at-risk accounts, personalize communication at scale, and deliver proactive value before customers ask.

---

### Engineering / Product
Priority: 3, 2, 8, 9, 1, 10
Bonus: 4, 5, 6, 7

**Role context**: Engineers and PMs are building BombBomb's product and internal systems. They're often more technically comfortable with AI but need to understand both the technical landscape and the organizational/business context. AI should accelerate development, improve decision-making, and help them understand what's possible.

---

### Leadership / Executive
Priority: 10, 3, 4, 7, 8, 9
Bonus: 1, 2, 5, 6

**Role context**: Leaders need to understand AI's strategic implications, make resourcing decisions, set policies, and model AI adoption for their teams. They don't necessarily need deep technical skills but do need solid mental models, awareness of risks, and the ability to evaluate AI initiatives and ROI.

---

### HR / People Operations
Priority: 4, 1, 5, 10, 9, 2
Bonus: 3, 6, 7, 8

**Role context**: HR teams are grappling with AI's impact on hiring, performance management, learning & development, and workplace policy. AI can help with job description writing, candidate screening assistance, policy documentation, and building internal learning programs. Privacy and ethics are especially important for this role.

---

### Support / Operations
Priority: 1, 4, 2, 8, 9, 6
Bonus: 3, 5, 7, 10

**Role context**: Support and ops teams handle high volumes of repetitive, structured tasks — ticket routing, documentation, reporting, process management. AI should dramatically reduce their administrative burden, improve response quality, and surface patterns in data that help improve systems and processes.

---

### Other
Priority: 1, 5, 9, 3, 4, 10
Bonus: 2, 6, 7, 8

**Role context**: A broad starting foundation covering prompting, creative work, the AI landscape, understanding agents, data privacy, and career development. Good for anyone who doesn't fit neatly into another category.

---

## Knowledge Level Guidance

### Beginner
- Avoid acronyms and jargon without plain-English explanation
- Lead with "why this matters" before "how it works"
- Focus on 2-3 things to try this week, not comprehensive coverage
- Use analogies and concrete examples constantly
- Tone: encouraging, low-pressure, "you've got this"
- Avoid: RAG, fine-tuning, embeddings, API, tokens (or explain if needed)

### Intermediate
- Assume familiarity with ChatGPT, Claude, or similar
- Focus on workflow integration and combining tools
- Include specific tool names and platforms
- Cover both the what and the how with practical depth
- Tone: collaborative, practical, peer-to-peer
- Good to include: prompt engineering techniques, automation platforms, tool comparison

### Advanced
- Assume strong AI literacy and daily usage
- Focus on optimization, tradeoffs, and architectural decisions
- Cover edge cases, limitations, and nuance
- Include emerging capabilities and where the field is heading
- Tone: technical peer, intellectually engaging
- Good to include: agentic architectures, fine-tuning considerations, evaluation frameworks, ROI measurement, building vs. buying decisions
