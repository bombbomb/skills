# BB Onboarding — Copy-Paste Prompt Library

Ready-to-use prompts organized by role. Each prompt is designed to be immediately useful for a BombBomb employee.

---

## Sales Prompts

### Prospect Research Brief
**When to use**: Before any important call or outreach
```
You are a B2B sales research assistant. I have a meeting with [NAME] at [COMPANY] on [DATE]. 

Please research:
1. What does [COMPANY] do and who are their customers?
2. What are their likely pain points around [customer communication / video / relationship-building]?
3. What recent news (funding, leadership changes, product launches) should I reference?
4. What questions should I open with to understand their situation?
5. How does BombBomb's video messaging platform likely fit their needs?

Keep it concise — I need a 2-minute brief I can read right before the call.
```

### Personalized Video Script
**When to use**: Writing outreach or follow-up video scripts
```
Write a 45-60 second video script for me to record to [NAME] at [COMPANY]. 

Context:
- We met at [EVENT / had a call about / I'm following up on]
- Their main challenge seems to be [CHALLENGE]
- I want to [propose a demo / share a resource / move to next step]

The script should sound like me talking, not a formal email read aloud. Conversational, warm, specific to them. End with a clear, low-friction ask.
```

### Objection Response
**When to use**: Preparing for or responding to common objections
```
A prospect just said: "[OBJECTION]"

I sell BombBomb, a video messaging platform that helps sales and CS teams build relationships through personal video. Our typical customer is [ICP].

Help me craft a thoughtful, non-defensive response that:
1. Acknowledges their concern genuinely
2. Reframes the objection with a different perspective
3. Offers a concrete next step

Keep it under 4 sentences — I'll say this on a call, not in an email.
```

### Follow-Up Sequence Builder
**When to use**: Building a multi-touch outreach sequence
```
Help me build a 5-touch outreach sequence for [PROSPECT TYPE] who [SITUATION].

For each touch, give me:
- Channel (email / video / LinkedIn / phone)
- Timing (Day 1, Day 3, etc.)
- Key message in 1-2 sentences
- A hook or subject line if email

The goal is to get a 30-minute discovery call. Tone should be human and relationship-focused — not spammy or high-pressure.
```

## Marketing Prompts

### Campaign Concept Generator
**When to use**: Kicking off a new campaign or content series
```
We're planning a marketing campaign for BombBomb targeting [AUDIENCE] with the goal of [GOAL].

Generate 5 distinct campaign concepts. For each:
- Concept name and one-line pitch
- Core message / insight it's built on
- Hero content format (video series, webinar, email, etc.)
- A sample headline or hook
- Why this would resonate with [AUDIENCE]

Our brand voice: human, warm, relationship-focused, never salesy. We believe video messaging builds the kind of authentic connections that email alone can't.
```

### Content Calendar Builder
**When to use**: Planning monthly content
```
Create a 4-week content calendar for BombBomb's [CHANNEL — LinkedIn / blog / email newsletter].

Our audience: [AUDIENCE DESCRIPTION]
This month's theme / focus: [THEME]
Posting cadence: [X times per week]

For each post, give me:
- Day and format (short-form, long-form, video, infographic idea, etc.)
- Topic and angle
- Opening hook (first sentence or two)

Keep ideas varied — mix education, inspiration, social proof, and direct value.
```

### Email Subject Line Testing
**When to use**: Before sending a campaign email
```
I'm sending an email campaign about [TOPIC] to [AUDIENCE].

Write 8 subject line options that maximize open rate. Include:
- 2 curiosity-driven
- 2 benefit-focused
- 2 ultra-short (under 5 words)
- 2 personalized / conversational

Then rank them 1-8 and explain your top pick in one sentence.
```

## Customer Success Prompts

### Customer Check-In Script
**When to use**: Preparing for a quarterly business review or check-in video
```
Write a 60-second video script for a customer check-in with [CUSTOMER NAME / COMPANY].

What I know about them:
- They've been a customer for [TIME]
- They use BombBomb primarily for [USE CASE]
- Recent context: [RECENT WIN / CHALLENGE / MILESTONE]

Goals for this video:
- Show I know their business, not just their account
- [Share a tip / Celebrate a win / Flag something proactively]
- Invite them to [a call / a new feature demo / leave a review]

Warm, personal, brief. Should feel like a call from someone who cares.
```

### At-Risk Account Analysis
**When to use**: When a customer shows warning signs
```
I'm concerned about [CUSTOMER NAME]. Here's what I know:

Usage: [DECLINING / FLAT / SPECIFIC METRICS]
Last contact: [DATE AND OUTCOME]
Their stated goals when they signed: [GOALS]
Recent changes at their company: [IF KNOWN]

Help me:
1. Identify the most likely root cause of disengagement
2. Suggest 2-3 re-engagement approaches ranked by likelihood of success
3. Draft a short, direct subject line for my outreach

Don't sugarcoat — I need to act fast.
```

## Engineering / Product Prompts

### Technical Spec Reviewer
**When to use**: Getting a second opinion on a technical document
```
Review the following technical specification and give me:
1. Ambiguities or missing information that will cause problems in implementation
2. Potential edge cases not addressed
3. Consistency issues or contradictions
4. One question I should answer before this goes to engineering

[PASTE SPEC]
```

### User Story Generator
**When to use**: Translating feature ideas into tickets
```
We're building [FEATURE] for BombBomb. Here's the context:
[DESCRIPTION OF THE FEATURE AND WHY WE WANT IT]

Generate 5-8 user stories in this format:
As a [user type], I want [capability] so that [benefit].

Then add acceptance criteria (3-5 bullet points) for the 2 most important stories.
```

## Leadership / Executive Prompts

### AI Initiative Evaluator
**When to use**: Evaluating a proposed AI project or vendor
```
Evaluate this AI initiative proposal through the lens of a skeptical but open-minded executive:

[PASTE PROPOSAL OR DESCRIBE INITIATIVE]

Give me:
1. The strongest argument FOR this initiative
2. The strongest argument AGAINST it
3. The 3 questions I should ask the proposer
4. What success looks like at 90 days if we proceed
5. One thing I'd want to know about the vendor / tool / approach before committing
```

### Board/Team AI Update
**When to use**: Communicating AI strategy to leadership or the board
```
Help me write a 5-slide narrative for an AI strategy update for [BOARD / LEADERSHIP TEAM / ALL-HANDS].

Context:
- Where we are with AI adoption today: [STATUS]
- Key initiatives underway: [LIST]
- Wins to highlight: [WINS]
- Risks we're managing: [RISKS]
- What we're asking for / deciding: [ASK OR DECISION]

For each slide: headline, 3 key points, and one data point or proof point if applicable. Tone: confident, grounded, not hype-y.
```

## HR / People Operations Prompts

### Job Description Enhancer
**When to use**: Writing or improving a job posting
```
Improve this job description to attract strong candidates while accurately representing the role:

[PASTE JD]

Please:
1. Rewrite the opening paragraph to lead with impact and culture, not requirements
2. Audit the requirements list — flag any that are likely screening out qualified candidates unnecessarily
3. Add 2-3 sentences about BombBomb's culture and what makes it a great place to work
4. Suggest a more compelling job title if appropriate

Keep the total length reasonable — candidates scan, they don't read.
```

### Interview Question Generator
**When to use**: Preparing a structured interview
```
Generate a structured interview guide for a [ROLE] position at BombBomb.

The role requires: [KEY SKILLS / COMPETENCIES]
Deal-breaker qualities to screen for: [LIST]
Culture fit indicators we care about: [LIST]

Include:
- 3 behavioral questions (with what you're listening for)
- 2 situational / hypothetical questions
- 1 role-specific technical or skills question
- 2 questions that help the candidate learn about us (not just us evaluating them)
```

## Support / Operations Prompts

### Process Documentation Writer
**When to use**: Turning a process in your head into written documentation
```
Help me write a clear process document for [PROCESS NAME].

Here's how the process works today (rough notes):
[YOUR ROUGH NOTES / BRAIN DUMP]

Please structure this as:
- Purpose (1-2 sentences on why this process exists)
- Who's responsible
- Step-by-step instructions (numbered, with decision points called out)
- Common mistakes to avoid
- What to do when something goes wrong

Write for someone doing this for the first time.
```

### Ticket / Issue Triage Helper
**When to use**: Categorizing and prioritizing a backlog of issues
```
Here are [N] support tickets / issues / tasks I need to triage:

[PASTE LIST]

Please:
1. Group them by category / theme
2. Flag any that seem urgent (affecting many users, blocking revenue, etc.)
3. Identify any that are duplicates or closely related
4. Suggest a priority order for the top 5

Assume we have limited bandwidth and need to focus on highest impact first.
```
